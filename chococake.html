Double-Chocolate Bundt Cake with Ganache Glaze

Dessert Ingredients Procedures

  • Vegetable Oil Spray
  • 5 ounces bittersweet chocolate (chopped)
  • ¾ cup canola oil
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1 large egg
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • ½ cup Dutch-process cocoa powder
  • 1 tablespoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup strong-brewed coffee
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • ⅓ cup heavy cream
  • ½ tablespoon corn syrup
  • ½ tablespoon unsalted butter

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Spray a 12-cup Bundt pan with vegetable oil spray. Melt 2 ounces of the chopped chocolate over low heat, stirring constantly. Let cool slightly. Whisk in the oil and sugar, then whisk in the egg.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk flour, cocoa powder, baking soda, and salt. Add half of the dry ingredients to the chocolate mixture, along with ½ cup coffee and ½ cup buttermilk. Whisk until smooth, then add the rest of the dry ingredients, coffee, and buttermilk.
  3. Pour the batter into the prepared pan and bake in the lower third of the oven for about 45 minutes or until a toothpick comes out clean. Let cool for 10 minutes, then turn out and let cool completely.
  4. In a saucepan, bring the cream to a boil. Combine the remaining chocolate with corn syrup and butter in a heatproof bowl. Pour the cream over the chocolate, then whisk until smooth. Let the ganache cool for about 5 minutes.
  5. Pour the ganache over the cooled cake. Let the glaze set for at least 30 minutes before serving.
